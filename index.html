Airlines’ Passenger Count from Asia

This chart shows the passenger count of each of the 21 most popular airlines that fly to Asia. The height of the bar represents the passenger count, so taller bars represent higher passenger count. Data was wrangled with Tableau.

From the data, it’s clear that United serves the most passengers between Asia and SFO.

SFO Number of Travelers by Region over Time, All flights

This area chart shows the number of passengers served between 2005 and 2009, broken down by geographical region served. The height of the region represents the passenger count for that year, so larger regions indicate more passengers. Data was wrangled with Tableau.

From the data, it’s clear that the U.S. is the most popular region served by SFO, while Asia is the most popular international region served.


SFO Number of International Travelers by Region and Airline

This chart shows the passenger count of international flights, broken down further by airline in a stacked bar chart. The x axis is the geographical region, and the y-axis is passenger count. Each airline has a color, indicated by the color legend. Data was wrangled with Tableau.

From the data, it’s clear that Alaskan Airways only serves international flights to Mexico and Canada.
